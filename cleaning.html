Cleaning

A clean bike is a happy bike, and a clean cycle is the best way to prevent the build-up of grime that can cause potential problems for components and make life harder for you.

Mountain Bike

Mountain bikes have different tires than street bikes, which makes them better at handling obstacles such as rocks and roots. They are built to handle big jumps and drops better than other bikes, but that doesn't mean they can handle everything. For example, mountain bikes don't perform well when riding on slick surfaces or going uphill.

Road Bike

Road bikes have been designed to be as fast as possible on pavement. As such, they are built with components that are optimized for speed over comfort or durability. They have small wheels, a skinny seat, and a handlebar stem that is positioned so that you can see over or around it. The riding position places your body directly over the front wheel, allowing you to lean over the bike as it goes around corners.
